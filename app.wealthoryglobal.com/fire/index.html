Financial Independence, Retire Early (FIRE) Calculator

The FIRE movement is about gaining the financial freedom to choose how you spend your time, rather than working out of necessity until traditional retirement age. To achieve this, you need to reach your "FIRE Number" — a corpus large enough to sustain your lifestyle indefinitely through passive income.

What is the FIRE Number and the 25x Rule?

Your FIRE Number is the total investment corpus required to generate enough passive income to cover your living expenses. A universally accepted rule of thumb in the FIRE community is the 25x Rule, which states you need 25 times your annual expenses saved. This is derived from the 4% Safe Withdrawal Rate (SWR) — the theory that if you withdraw 4% of your portfolio in the first year of retirement and adjust for inflation thereafter, your money will last indefinitely.

Why does this calculator factor in Inflation?

If you plan to FIRE in 10 or 15 years, your expenses will not be the same as they are today. At an average Indian inflation rate of 6%, an expense of Rs 50,000 per month today will double in roughly 12 years. Our calculator first inflates your current expenses to their future equivalent at your target FIRE age, and then calculates the 25x corpus based on that future expense. This ensures your FIRE plan is robust and realistic.

How does my current savings affect my SIP?

If you already have investments, they will continue to compound until your FIRE age. The calculator projects the future value of your current savings and subtracts it from your Target FIRE Corpus. The remaining amount (the shortfall) determines the monthly SIP you need to start today. The larger your current savings, the smaller your required SIP.
