Income Tax Calculator India 2025: Old vs New Regime

Choosing between the old and new tax regimes is one of the most important financial decisions for salaried individuals and self-employed professionals in India. The new tax regime, introduced in Budget 2020 and significantly revised in subsequent budgets, offers lower tax slab rates but eliminates most deductions and exemptions. Our income tax calculator compares both regimes side by side so you can make an informed choice.

Understanding the New Tax Regime Slabs

The new tax regime for FY 2025-26 features a simplified slab structure: income up to Rs 3 lakh is exempt from tax, income between Rs 3-6 lakh is taxed at 5%, Rs 6-9 lakh at 10%, Rs 9-12 lakh at 15%, Rs 12-15 lakh at 20%, and any income above Rs 15 lakh is taxed at 30%. A standard deduction of Rs 75,000 is available under the new regime. This structure benefits individuals who do not have significant deductions to claim.

When Should You Choose the Old Regime?

The old tax regime is beneficial when your total deductions and exemptions exceed approximately Rs 3.75 lakh to Rs 4 lakh. This includes deductions under Section 80C (up to Rs 1.5 lakh for ELSS, PPF, EPF, etc.), Section 80D (health insurance premiums), HRA exemption for salaried individuals paying rent, home loan interest under Section 24, and NPS contributions under Section 80CCD. If you are a disciplined investor who maximizes these deductions every year, the old regime may save you significant tax.

How to Use This Calculator

Simply enter your annual gross income and your deduction amounts using the sliders. The calculator instantly computes your tax liability under both regimes, shows a slab-by-slab breakdown, and recommends the regime that results in the lowest tax. The doughnut chart provides a visual comparison, making it easy to understand the tax impact at a glance. Review the results and plan your investments accordingly to optimize your tax outflow.

  • Compare both regimes before filing your ITR
  • Maximize Section 80C investments for old regime benefits
  • Consider health insurance under Section 80D for additional savings
  • Factor in HRA exemption if you live in rented accommodation
  • Consult a tax professional for complex income structures
