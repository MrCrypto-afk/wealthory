Retirement Calculator India: Plan a Worry-Free Future

Retirement planning is one of the most important financial decisions you will make. With rising life expectancy and increasing healthcare costs, building a sufficient retirement corpus has never been more critical. Our retirement calculator helps you estimate exactly how much you need to save and invest to maintain your current lifestyle after you stop working.

How Does the Retirement Corpus Calculator Work?

This calculator uses three key inputs to determine your retirement needs: your current monthly expenses, expected inflation rate, and the number of years until retirement. It first projects your monthly expenses at the time of retirement by adjusting for inflation using the compound growth formula. Then, assuming a 25-year retirement period, it calculates the total corpus required to sustain those inflation-adjusted expenses throughout your retirement years.

Why Inflation Matters in Retirement Planning

Many people underestimate the impact of inflation on their retirement needs. At an average inflation rate of 6%, prices double approximately every 12 years. This means if you currently spend Rs 50,000 per month, you could need over Rs 2 lakh per month by the time you retire in 25 years. Ignoring inflation is the single biggest mistake in retirement planning, and our calculator ensures you account for it accurately.

Start Your SIP Early for Maximum Benefit

The power of compounding works best over long periods. Starting a systematic investment plan (SIP) in your twenties can dramatically reduce the monthly amount you need to invest compared to starting in your thirties or forties. Even a small SIP started early can grow into a substantial retirement corpus over two to three decades. Use the sliders above to see how your required SIP changes based on your current age and target retirement age.

  • Account for inflation when estimating future expenses
  • Plan for at least 25 years of retirement
  • Review and increase your SIP annually
  • Diversify across equity, debt, and gold for balanced returns
  • Consider a retirement-focused mutual fund for disciplined investing
