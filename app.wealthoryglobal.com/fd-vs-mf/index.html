Fixed Deposits vs. Mutual Funds: A Financial Analysis

The debate between Fixed Deposits (FDs) and Equity Mutual Funds is the cornerstone of personal finance. This calculator helps you mathematically compare the post-tax, inflation-adjusted returns of both asset classes over your specific time horizon.

Understanding the Trade-off

Fixed Deposits provide absolute capital protection and a guaranteed interest rate. However, their post-tax returns often fail to beat inflation, resulting in a loss of purchasing power. Mutual Funds carry market risk and volatility, but historically generate the highest real (inflation-adjusted) returns over 10+ year periods.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to move money from FD to Mutual Funds?

If your investment horizon is longer than 5 to 7 years, shifting to equity mutual funds is generally recommended to beat inflation. For money needed within 1 to 3 years, FDs remain the safer choice.

How does taxation affect FDs vs MFs?

FD interest is typically taxed according to your income tax slab every year. Equity Mutual Funds are taxed only upon withdrawal, often at a lower Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG) rate, making MFs much more tax-efficient.
