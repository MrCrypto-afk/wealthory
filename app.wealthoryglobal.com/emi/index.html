The Mechanics of Debt Amortisation

Understanding your Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) is critical for effective liability management. Our institutional-grade calculator uses standard amortisation algorithms to split your payments between principal reduction and interest capitalization.

How does Loan Interest work?

Most retail loans operate on a reducing balance basis. When a loan begins, the outstanding principal is at its highest, meaning the interest component of your EMI is also at its peak. Over time, as the principal decreases, the interest portion shrinks and the principal repayment portion accelerates.

The EMI Formula Architecture

EMI = P × r × (1+r)n / ((1+r)n - 1)

Where:
P = Principal Loan Amount
r = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate / 12 / 100)
n = Loan Tenure in Months

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an EMI?

An Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) is the fixed sum of money you pay a financial institution each month to clear a debt. It covers both the interest accrued for that month and a portion of the original capital borrowed.

How can I reduce my total interest outflow?

You can significantly reduce interest costs by either negotiating a lower interest rate, reducing the tenure of the loan (which increases monthly EMI but decreases total interest), or making regular prepayments towards the principal amount.
