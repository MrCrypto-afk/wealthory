The Hidden Cost of Waiting to Invest

Many investors believe they can simply "catch up later" when their income increases. However, the mathematics of compounding interest proves that time is far more valuable than the principal amount invested. This calculator reveals the massive invisible penalty you pay by delaying your investment journey.

How Delay Destroys Wealth

Compounding is exponential, which means the most explosive growth happens in the final years of an investment timeline. By delaying your start date, you are permanently cutting off those final, most lucrative years of compounding from your timeline.

The Financial Impact

Cost of Delay = Future Value (Starting Today) - Future Value (Starting Later)

Every year you delay investing a standard SIP, you lose not just the principal you would have invested, but the decades of compound interest that principal would have generated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I catch up by investing more later?

You can, but it requires significantly more capital. To match the final corpus of someone who started 10 years earlier, you often have to invest 3 to 4 times as much money out of pocket.

When is the best time to start investing?

The famous proverb applies: "The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago. The second best time is today." Start immediately, even with a very small amount.
