Lumpsum Investment Growth Mechanics

A lumpsum investment allows your entire capital base to begin compounding immediately. For investors who receive an inheritance, a large bonus, or sell an asset, a lumpsum investment often yields higher absolute returns over long timeframes compared to staggering the money.

The Power of Uninterrupted Compounding

Future Value = P × (1 + r)n

Where:
P = Initial Principal Amount
r = Annual Rate of Return
n = Number of Years

Frequently Asked Questions

Is lumpsum better than SIP?

Mathematically, if the market trends upwards over time, lumpsum beats SIP because all your money is invested for longer. Psychologically and for risk-management, SIP is often preferred to avoid investing everything right before a market crash.

Should I wait for a market crash to invest a lumpsum?

No. "Time in the market beats timing the market." Studies show that waiting for a dip often results in lower returns than simply investing the lumpsum immediately, because the market often goes up while you wait.
